Sound Byte

This was a simple UI design for an Android app, Sound Byte, completed in 24 hours and the winning entry in the 2013 Accessibility Hackathon. Our team elected to create an intelligent 'hearing aid' app for those with hearing loss who choose not to use a conventional hearing aid. We modified an algorithm created by developer Nick Clark for the iPhone app Bioaid, endeavouring to create a more usable app with a broader functionality whose main user interface elements were simply an on button and a volume control, pictured on the first screenshot.

The second screen represents a user interface design for the settings page of the app, which takes into account the individual hearing loss of a user. This was a substantial redesign of the original Bioaid app which aimed to simplify the rather overwhelming list of settings.

I adopted a skeuomorphic approach for this design primarily because, as a pre-iOS 7 iPhone user, I assumed this to be standard practice for Android user interfaces too. It was not until after the 24 hour hackathon that I realised Android design tends to be much flatter and more minimalist, which will be taken into account in any further development of the app (and has now become the standard for iPhone app design too)!
